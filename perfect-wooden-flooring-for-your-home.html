Why Wooden Flooring?

Wooden flooring is one of the most sought-after choices for homeowners due to its warmth, charm, and versatile aesthetics. It effortlessly blends with both traditional and contemporary home designs. Additionally, wood floors are incredibly durable, long-lasting, and can enhance the resale value of your property. A variety of wood species, finishes, and colors allow you to customize your flooring to suit your preferences, making it easy to match with your existing decor.

Types of Wooden Flooring

Before you dive into the details of finishes and installation methods, it's essential to understand the different types of wooden flooring available. The main types include solid hardwood, engineered wood, and laminate wood, each of which has its pros and cons.



Solid Hardwood Flooring

Solid hardwood flooring is made from a single piece of wood and is available in various species like oak, maple, cherry, walnut, and more. It is known for its durability and natural beauty. The thickness of solid hardwood can range from ¾ inch to 5/8 inch, and it can be sanded and refinished multiple times, allowing you to restore its appearance for years.

The classic appeal of solid hardwood makes it an ideal choice for almost any room in your home. However, one potential downside is that solid wood is sensitive to humidity and temperature changes, which can cause it to warp or expand. Therefore, it’s better suited for controlled environments like living rooms or hallways.



Engineered Wood Flooring

Engineered wood flooring is made from multiple layers of wood, with a thin veneer of solid hardwood on top. This type of flooring is more stable than solid hardwood, making it suitable for installation in areas with fluctuating temperatures and humidity levels, such as basements or kitchens. Crescent Furnishings offers engineered wood options in a variety of finishes, providing the beauty of hardwood with added practicality.

Engineered wood comes in a variety of thicknesses and widths, with the top layer being a high-quality veneer that mimics the look of solid wood. It can be sanded and refinished, but not as many times as solid hardwood. Nevertheless, engineered wood is an excellent choice for those looking for a blend of durability and elegance.



Laminate Wood Flooring

Laminate wood flooring offers a cost-effective alternative to hardwood and engineered wood. Made from fiberboard with a high-resolution image of wood on top, laminate flooring provides the aesthetic of real wood without the hefty price tag. While laminate is durable and easy to maintain, it lacks the authentic look and feel of genuine wood.

Although laminate wood flooring doesn't have the same resale value or luxury appeal as hardwood or engineered wood, it’s still a popular choice for many homeowners on a budget. It can be installed easily, and with advances in technology, the latest laminate flooring can mimic the appearance of real wood with remarkable accuracy.



Maintenance Tips for Wooden Floors

Wooden floors are relatively low-maintenance, but they do require regular care to maintain their beauty. Here are some maintenance tips to keep your floors in top condition:


  • Clean your wooden floors regularly with a soft broom or vacuum to remove dust and debris.
  • Avoid using harsh cleaning chemicals that can damage the finish.
  • Wipe up spills immediately to prevent moisture from seeping into the wood.
  • Place area rugs or mats in high-traffic areas to protect the floor from scratches.
  • Periodically polish or re-oil the floor depending on the type of finish to maintain its luster.
